\subsection{Experiment 1: Encoding Layer Validation}

The multimodal encoding layer achieves an average fidelity of \AVGFIDELITY{} across knowledge types (Figure~\ref{fig:fidelity}).
Policy encoding is lossless (\FIDELITYPOLICY{}), expert judgment encoding preserves all semantic content (\FIDELITYEXPERT{}), and quantitative encoding achieves \FIDELITYQUANT{} fidelity---the slight loss reflecting dimensionality reduction in distribution fitting.

Cross-type query performance strongly differentiates the encoding approach from baselines (Figure~\ref{fig:crosstype}).
The full system achieves \CROSSTYPEACCURACY{} accuracy on queries requiring reasoning across two or more knowledge types, compared to \RAWCONCATACCURACY{} for raw concatenation and \LLMBASELINEACCURACY{} for the LLM baseline.
The encoding layer's preservation of epistemic metadata enables the cross-encoder to correctly weigh conflicting signals---a capability that raw text concatenation cannot support.

The system detected \CONFLICTSDETECTED{}/\CONFLICTSTOTAL{} planted cross-source conflicts, including the price-promotion trap where expert judgment contradicts joint quantitative analysis, and the constraint-coupling conflict where expert recommendations exceed policy margin thresholds.

\subsection{Experiment 2: Ablation Study}

The ablation study demonstrates that each structural invariant contributes to system performance (Figure~\ref{fig:ablation}, Table~\ref{tab:ablation}).

The full system discovers \EFFECTSDISCOVERED{}/\EFFECTSTOTAL{} ground-truth effects with a precision of \FULLPRECISION{} and recall of \FULLRECALL{}.
Removing the coupling model (\textit{No Coupling}) reduces effects discovered to \NOCOUPLINGEFFECTS{}, as interaction effects GT-1 (price-promotion) and GT-2 (assortment-distribution) require cross-lever analysis.
Removing multimodal encoding (\textit{No Encoding}) reduces precision, as the system cannot properly weigh conflicting evidence without epistemic metadata.
Removing the progressive pipeline (\textit{No Pipeline}) yields the lowest precision at \NOPIPELINEPRECISION{}, confirming that unstructured single-pass analysis cannot systematically explore the decision space.

\subsection{Experiment 3: Progressive Reduction Pipeline}

The pipeline achieves dramatic compression while preserving ground-truth coverage at every stage (Figure~\ref{fig:compression}).
Stage~1 (diagnostic agents) reduces the ${\sim}10^{18}$ input space to \PIPELINESTAGE{1} evidence packets.
Stage~2 (causal synthesis) consolidates to \PIPELINESTAGE{2} structured interventions.
Stage~3 (quality gate) selects \PIPELINEOUTPUT{} final recommendations with zero hard-constraint violations.

Ground-truth coverage remains at \GTCOVERAGE{3} through all stages, confirming that the compression is lossless with respect to the planted interaction effects.

The quality profile of the top interventions (Figure~\ref{fig:radar}) shows strong performance across all scoring dimensions, with perfect constraint alignment and high evidence density.
